## Craig Newmark Philanthropies — Company Profile

Craig Newmark Philanthropies (CNP) is the charitable organization founded by web pioneer Craig Newmark (founder of craigslist). The philanthropy focuses full‑time on supporting organizations that protect America’s security and democracy, with major commitments to veterans and military families, trustworthy (nonpartisan) journalism, and cybersecurity education. CNP’s grants also span hunger relief and animal welfare (including pigeon rescue). According to its newsroom, Newmark has contributed more than $500 million across these areas.

Core focus and what they fund
- Democracy and national security
  - Support for organizations “protecting America’s security and democracy,” including efforts to counter disinformation and bolster civic resilience.
- Trustworthy journalism
  - Sustaining nonpartisan news and strengthening the press as an “immune system of democracy.”
- Veterans and military families
  - Grants and programs aiding service members, veterans, and their families.
- Cybersecurity and cyber education
  - Investments in cyber workforce development and education (e.g., initiatives supporting Gen Z cyber defense).
- Additional causes
  - Hunger relief and animal welfare.

About Craig Newmark
- Web pioneer best known for founding craigslist; stepped back from craigslist day‑to‑day operations two decades ago to focus on philanthropy.
- Publicly articulates overarching principles that guide giving: treat people as you’d like to be treated; fight “false witness” (disinformation); uphold rule of law and equality under the law; and sustain a trustworthy press.
- Often describes the current moment as an “information war,” emphasizing the need for civilian engagement and institutional resilience.

Recent mentions (as highlighted in the newsroom)
- Recognition by civic groups (e.g., NYC Jewish Community Relations Council).
- Features and interviews on the origin of craigslist, philanthropy motivations, and investments in cybersecurity and youth cyber defense.
- Commentary on combating misinformation and building connections through technology.

Distinctive aspects
- Founder‑led philanthropy with a focused portfolio on democracy protection, high‑impact journalism, and cyber defense.
- A publicly stated cumulative giving figure of $500M+ across priority areas.
- Emphasis on principles derived from personal history and mentors (e.g., Sunday School teachers who fled the Holocaust), which shape the philanthropy’s mission alignment and approach.
