## Seattle Partners — Startup Consulting for Launch, Growth, and U.S. Market Entry

Seattle Partners is a Seattle-based startup consulting firm that helps early-stage and scaling companies launch, grow, and expand—especially into the U.S. market. The firm emphasizes product- and value-focused execution, combining market research, product management support, go-to-market planning, and business expansion services. It curates programs and resources from the Greater Seattle ecosystem and partners with regional institutions to accelerate startups’ progress.

Core focus and services
- Startup consulting across the startup journey: product, marketing, business development, organization, and operations (About).
- Research services: market research, competitive intelligence, customer research, and product research (About).
- Services menu includes:
  - PMF (product–market fit) research
  - POC and business model validation
  - GTM strategy and planning
  - Workshops and events
  - Business expansion
  - Access to funding (Services menu)
- Programs menu highlights:
  - U.S. Market GTM Playbook
  - ECHO – remote U.S. market research for global startups
  - Sandbox – U.S. business operations environment for global startups
  - Gateway to the U.S. Market Entry (Programs menu)

Target customers and industries
- Early-stage and scaling startups, with a clear emphasis on global startups seeking U.S. market entry.
- Coverage of technology-driven sectors; example cohorts include pet healthcare solutions/services and retail products with technology/design innovation (Homepage highlights).

Unique value proposition
- Seattle-centric platform: situates clients in a top-tier innovation environment (University of Washington and CoMotion Startup Hall), offering facilities, advisors, and network access to support scale-up (Homepage, About).
- Practical, end-to-end approach: combines research, modeling, and strategic execution guidance with targeted engagement to surface and capture growth opportunities (About).
- Ecosystem navigation: actively curates and signals relevant accelerator opportunities and cloud/startup programs (e.g., Techstars Seattle, Microsoft for Startups Founders Hub, Google for Startups Cloud Program) and is “ready to talk to companies” that may benefit (News section).

Notable partnerships and initiatives

- Programs and events:
