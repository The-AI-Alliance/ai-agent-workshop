## Cleveland Clinic — Organization Overview

Cleveland Clinic is a nonprofit, multispecialty academic medical center that integrates clinical and hospital care with research and education. Founded in 1921 as a group practice, it has grown into one of the world’s largest healthcare systems with a global footprint. Its mission remains “caring for life, researching for health, and educating those who serve,” delivered through a patient‑centered, team‑based model that emphasizes measurable quality, innovation, and continuous improvement.

Core focus and services
- Comprehensive clinical care: Multispecialty inpatient and outpatient services, surgeries and procedures, emergency care, and virtual care.
- Academic medicine: One of the nation’s largest physician‑graduate training programs; integrated institutes aligned to organ systems/disease areas.
- Research and innovation: Large research enterprise (Lerner Research Institute), clinical trials, and a long history of medical firsts and technology breakthroughs.
- Public reporting: Transparent reporting of treatment outcomes and quality metrics to drive accountability and improvement.

Scale and reach (2024 figures)
- System size: 23 hospitals, 280 outpatient locations, 6,728 beds.
- Patients: 3.47M unique patients; 15.66M patient encounters worldwide; 1.11M virtual visits.
- Caregivers: 82,608 worldwide, including 5,786 physicians/scientists, 16,845 registered nurses, and 3,902 advanced practice providers.
- Education and research: 2,369 clinical/research residents and fellows; 126 accredited residency/training programs; $520M total research funding (including $156M from NIH); 3,655 active research projects.
- Financials: $15.94B operating revenue; $275.7M income from operations; 1.7% operating margin.
- Community & economic impact (2023): $1.46B in community benefit; U.S. economic impact of $37.59B (Ohio: $28.19B; Florida: $4.39B; Nevada: $0.08B).

Model and differentiators
- Group practice model: Physicians are salaried employees (not in private practice), reinforcing collaboration, standardization, and patient‑first decisions.
- Institute structure: Integrated practice units organized by organ system/disease to streamline care and improve patient experience.
- Patient‑centered care: Emphasis on listening, empathy, and tailoring care plans; multidisciplinary teams and collaborative providers.
- Measurable quality: Publicly posted treatment outcomes and a persistent focus on standards, data collection, and analysis.
- Innovation legacy: Pioneering cardiac surgery, angiography, transplantation; first U.S. near‑total face transplant; brain‑mapping to localize seizures; DBS to restore function after stroke; advancing vaccines for triple‑negative breast cancer, among other notable firsts.

Recognition
- Newsweek: Ranked No. 2 hospital in the world (2023) for the fifth consecutive year; multiple international facilities recognized among top hospitals.
- U.S. quality ratings: CMS five‑star ratings at several hospitals; Leapfrog Group A grades; Vizient top‑decile performance at multiple sites.
- Nursing excellence: 11 Cleveland Clinic hospitals with Magnet® designation.

Regions served and workforce
- Global presence with major concentrations in Northeast Ohio (largest private employer), as well as significant care delivery in Florida and Nevada, and international locations (e.g., Cleveland Clinic Abu Dhabi).

Careers and professional resources
- Careers: One of the world’s leading academic medical centers with extensive opportunities across clinical and non‑clinical roles.
- Provider resources: Referring physician support, alumni association, research/education programs, and professional learning via ConsultQD.

Distinctive aspects
- Transparent outcomes reporting and a salaried, team‑based physician model are central to its identity and quality focus.
- Unusually large scale in both clinical operations and academic medicine, with research, education, and care tightly integrated.
- Proven track record of medical innovation and major “firsts” that have shaped modern clinical practice.
