## Montreal AI Ethics Institute (MAIEI) — Organization Overview

The Montreal AI Ethics Institute (MAIEI) is an international non‑profit founded in 2018 by the late Abhishek Gupta and Renjie Butalid. Its core mission is to democratize AI ethics literacy by equipping citizens, policymakers, and industry leaders with the knowledge to navigate AI’s societal impacts and shape a more responsible, ethical future of AI.

Core focus and programs
- Education
  - The AI Ethics Brief: A bi‑weekly newsletter (15,000+ subscribers) summarizing the latest research and reporting in AI ethics.
  - AI Ethics Living Dictionary: A plain‑language glossary bridging technical AI and social‑science terminology for a general audience.
  - State of AI Ethics Reports (SAIER): In‑depth overviews of policy, research, risks, and emerging issues (latest: Volume 7 and a Special Edition).
- Advocacy
  - Public consultations: Ensures public voice is represented in policymaker decisions about AI.
  - Public interest initiatives: Supports the Montreal Declaration for Responsible Development of AI and European Digital Rights’ call for AI “red lines” in EU AI proposals.
- Community
  - Learning Community: Cohort‑based engagement for deeper study and discussion of AI ethics topics.

Values and model
- Accessibility and Open Access: All content is free to access and adapt; MAIEI avoids paywalls and clickbait, emphasizing concise, useful content.
- Reader privacy: Uses privacy‑respecting analytics (Fathom) instead of ad‑tracking models; does not sell readers’ data.
- Ethical revenue generation: Operates via donations and sponsored content, allowing focus on substantive educational content (e.g., research summaries) rather than clicks.

Funding and support
- Donations and paid subscriptions to the newsletter help sustain programming and scholarships honoring the legacy of co‑founder Abhishek Gupta.
- Corporate sponsorships are invited to support responsible AI governance initiatives.

Publications and media
- MAIEI curates regular Insights & Perspectives, Research Summaries, and The AI Ethics Brief, and maintains a Press page cataloging media mentions and appearances.

Distinctive aspects
- Clear emphasis on civic competence as a foundation for AI governance.
- Strong open‑access, privacy‑first ethos (no paywalls; privacy‑preserving analytics).
- Practical educational tools at scale (newsletter with 15k+ subscribers and a Living Dictionary) complemented by public consultation work.
